Japanese II

Before Japanese Class: I didn't think I knew much Japanese. I thought I would be socially-anxious or disorganized and present myself poorly. I expected to be overwhelmed.

After Japanese Class: I realized I knew more Japanese than I thought. Or maybe the logic was overwhelmingly sensible to me.

Learning Japanese made me even more excited about learning all of the languages of the Pacific Rim region, especially Hangeul and Cantonese.

I got an A in both classes!

HAI!

Calculus 3

Before the class I thought I could easily review the syllabus and easily prepare for the course by preparing for the lectures.

After the class I came to understand that I would have felt better prepared if I had prepared for the bulk of the course in advance of the semester.

Thankfully, the topic of decision making has always interested me. I can't wait to revisit the material.

Fiction Writing

Sometimes I want to be an author so badly, I can almost taste the ink.

Before the class, I was over-confident that I could produce material and peer review others stories capably.

Now that the class is over, I just want to take it again-- and the prerequisities.

I got a great grade, but I felt that spending ten years between creative writing classes was too much time spent lacking in the appropriate exchange of literary vocabulary.

Needless to say, I was rapt when it came to my professor's and my peers' analyses and opinions,

but I felt discombobulated when the opportunity came for me to return the favor.
